The philosophy

Before the program, one question: what's the limitation?

Close to a decade across CrossFit, weightlifting, HYROX, endurance, and nutrition taught me one thing: serious athletes don't stall for lack of effort — they stall for lack of direction.

So I don't start with a workout. I start by finding the one thing actually capping your level — the weakness, the gap, the habit — and I build everything around removing it. That range is the toolkit. It lets me read an athlete from more angles than a single-lane coach ever could.

Owning a real box keeps me honest about it. I coach on the floor every week — the same eye goes into your plan, whether you train in front of me or remotely.

Return stronger from injury.

Coming back from an injury is where most athletes lose the most ground — rushing the return, re-injuring, or training around the problem instead of through it. This is exactly where individualized coaching matters most.

  • A plan built around your limitation — not a generic comeback template.
  • Progressive load, monitored by a person who adjusts as you respond.
  • Rebuild capacity and confidence so you come back better than before — not just back.
